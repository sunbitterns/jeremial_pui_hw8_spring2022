abide by the rules.

barricade the classroom door.

close your mouth. stay quiet.

do not make a sound.

every day students recite this chant,

fervently and firm. dazed, they

give way to routine—

how comforting it is!

idyllic days stretch onward, as

joyful lessons

kidnap uneasiness and

lay it to rest.

miles of tangled worries are knit

neatly into a blindfold.

obey the rules. don’t

panic, don’t talk. don’t

question the rules. this is how we

reassemble safety and

sensibility.

targets on backs go

unaddressed. they are the

victims of blissful ignorance, too

weary to feign alarm when

x-ray machines start transforming into doors.

yesterday’s rationality dissolves. today is the

zenith of disarray.
